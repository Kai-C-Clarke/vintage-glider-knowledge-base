# Resorcinol Adhesives for Vintage Glider Repair — Current Position
## Compiled June 2026 | Source: BGA Technical News Sheet Issue 2-2025 (21 November 2025)

---

## Summary

Resorcinol adhesives remain the correct choice for primary structural wood repairs on vintage gliders.
The product landscape has changed. This note records the current position for inspectors and restorers.

---

## Aerodux 500 / 501 — DISCONTINUED

Aerodux 500 (liquid resin) with Hardener 501 (liquid hardener), mixed 1:1 by volume, is no longer
in production. This was the standard workshop product for many years and is referred to in older
references including Stafford Allen (1959) and various manufacturer maintenance manuals.

**Source:** BGA TNS Issue 2-2025, Safety Information Item 05:
"Aerodux prefer 500/501 no longer in production"

---

## Aerodux 185 — CURRENT APPROVED PRODUCT

Aerodux 185 (liquid phenol-resorcinol resin) mixed with powder hardener HRP.155 is the current
product mandated by the major glider manufacturers for wood repairs.

**Mixing ratio:** 5 parts resin to 1 part powder hardener (by weight)
**Hardener variants:**
- HRP.150 — specified by Scheibe aircraft
- HRP.155 — specified by Schempp-Hirth, Schleicher, CEAPRA Robin, and most others

**BGA TNS Issue 2-2025 states:**
"Scheibe, Schempp-Hirth, Schleicher and CEAPRA Robin all mandate using Aerodux 185 for wood
repairs. The hardener now comes in a powder form, is either 150 for Scheibe aircraft and most the
others appear to recommend 155. Read the datasheet for clarity on mixing ratios, pot life and
shelf life. Making a sample first is recommended if you have never used this version before."

**Standards compliance:** EN 301 Type 1, BS 1203 (Type WBP), DIN 68 141
**Luftfahrt-Bundesamt approved** for glued wood products in aeroplanes.

## Notes for Inspectors

1. Check tins are date-stamped and in-date before use. Resorcinol resins have a finite shelf life.

2. If using Aerodux 185 with powder hardener for the first time, make a test piece first.
   The powder system behaves differently to the old liquid-liquid 500/501 system.

3. Aerolite 306 (urea-formaldehyde) is NOT a substitute for resorcinol in primary structural joints.
   Aerolite is water-resistant (MR grade); resorcinol is fully waterproof (WBP grade).
   Use resorcinol for all primary structural repairs. Use Aerolite only for non-structural,
   interior, water-resistant work.

4. West System epoxy (105/205) is suitable for gap filling and fairing only — NOT primary structure
   on wood gliders. Some manufacturers explicitly prohibit epoxy for structural repairs.

5. Curing temperature matters. Resorcinol requires a minimum curing temperature — check the
   current datasheet. Cold workshops will extend cure time significantly.
