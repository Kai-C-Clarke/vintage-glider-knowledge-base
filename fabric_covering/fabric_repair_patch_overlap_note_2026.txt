# Fabric Repair Patch Overlap — Supplementary Note
## Compiled June 2026 | Sources: Ceconite Manual 101; BGA inspector practice (Jack)

---

## Ceconite Manual 101 — Appendix E Minimum Requirements

The Ceconite Manual 101 (June 2008 revision) states the following minimum overlaps
for repair patches:

- **Hole 8 inches or less:** minimum 1 inch overlap of patch over old fabric on all sides.
  Finishing tapes not required unless patch is on top of a wing.

- **Hole 8 inches or more:** minimum 2 inch overlap of patch over old fabric on all sides.
  Minimum 2 inch finishing tape centred over all seams required.

Cement: New Super Seam only (required under the Ceconite STC).

Iron temperatures:
- 225°F — heat-smooth cemented areas
- 250°F — heat-shrink patch over hole
- Do NOT exceed 250°F over cemented areas (risk of bond release)

---

## BGA Inspector Practice — Overlap for Unsupported Fabric

The Ceconite manual minimums apply to supported structure (fabric over ribs, over tubing).
For **unsupported fabric** — that is, fabric spanning between ribs with no structure
beneath the repair area — BGA inspector practice calls for greater overlap:

- **Supported structure** (fabric over rib, longeron, tube): 2 inch overlap minimum
- **Unsupported structure** (inter-rib span, open bay): 3 inch overlap minimum

This exceeds the Ceconite manual minimum and reflects the reduced backing available
to hold the cement bond in an unsupported panel. It is sound conservative practice
and should be applied on vintage glider fabric repairs.

**Source:** Oral tradition, BGA inspector community.

## Practical Repair Sequence — Inter-Rib Panel (Unsupported)

1. Trim ragged edges from damage — cut back to sound fabric
2. Clean all coatings from repair area down to bare fabric using butyrate thinner
   **WARNING: Never use paint stripper on Ceconite — releases seams and leaves paraffin**
3. Cut patch with pinking shears — square or rectangular
   Allow minimum 3 inch overlap onto sound fabric on all sides (unsupported span)
4. Pre-coat substrate with New Super Seam cement; allow to tack
5. Cement patch into place; work through with fingertips
6. Heat-smooth with 225°F iron
7. Heat-shrink with 250°F iron — this re-tightens the fabric in the repair area
8. Apply Rand-O-Proof (or non-tautening nitrate dope equivalent)
9. Apply finishing tape minimum 2 inch width centred over all seams
10. Rebuild dope system: nitrate → butyrate → butyrate aluminium → topcoat
11. Topcoat — cellulose or 2K as per airframe scheme
    Spray rib-to-rib for best colour match (Ceconite Appendix E recommendation)

## Form 205 Wording

> "Fabric repair to [component] in accordance with Ceconite Manual 101 Appendix E.
> Patch overlap 3 inches (unsupported structure). New Super Seam cement.
> Finished nitrate/butyrate/butyrate aluminium/[topcoat system]."
