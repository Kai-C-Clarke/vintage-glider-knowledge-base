# Polyester Fabric Covering — Reference Documentation Note
## Compiled June 2026 | Source: BGA inspector practice, oral tradition

---

## Summary

This note clarifies which reference document to cite when recovering a vintage glider
with polyester fabric, and why the Ceconite Manual 101 is the accepted reference
regardless of which brand of polyester fabric is actually used.

---

## The Accepted Reference Document

**Ceconite Manual 101** is the BGA-recognised reference document for polyester fabric
covering procedures on non-Part 21 sailplanes.

When completing BGA Form 205 (Rectification Worksheet) for a fabric recovering job,
the correct wording is:

> "Covered in accordance with Ceconite Manual 101"

This wording is accepted by BGA inspectors and the CAA regardless of whether the
fabric used is Ceconite, Diatex, or another equivalent polyester fabric product.
The process principles are common across all polyester fabric systems:
- Polyester fabric (heat-shrinkable)
- Non-tautening nitrate adhesive/sealant as first coat
- Butyrate dope over-coats
- Primer and topcoat finish

---

## Why Ceconite Manual 101 — Not the Diatex Procedure

Diatex publish their own covering procedure on their website (diatex.com), using their
proprietary Solo E.4040 nitrocellulose coating throughout. This is a valid system,
certified for CEAPR/Robin aircraft, but it is not the document recognised by the BGA
for Form 205 purposes.

Ceconite Manual 101 is the established reference that BGA inspectors and the CAA
recognise. It covers polyester fabric, non-tautening nitrate sealant, butyrate finish
coats, and topcoat systems — which matches the procedure used by the majority of
UK vintage glider restorers.

---

## Typical UK Vintage Glider Covering Procedure (Diatex with Nitrate/Butyrate)

The following reflects established UK practice as used by BGA inspectors.
It is documented here as a record of practitioner knowledge, not as approved
maintenance data. Always verify against current BGA guidance and your inspector.

1. Prepare surface — sand, clean, remove all grease and old finish
2. Apply nitrate adhesive (non-tautening) to wood/tube substrate
3. Apply Diatex (or equivalent polyester fabric) — position, stretch, pin
4. Apply nitrate adhesive through fabric to bond to substrate
5. Heat-shrink fabric with covering iron — work both faces, raise temperature
   progressively. Do not over-tauten. Max 180°C.
   **Caution: polyester fabric exerts significant force when tautening —
   do not over-shrink or airframe distortion may result.**
6. Apply further coats of non-tautening nitrate dope — typically 2-3 coats
7. Apply butyrate dope coats — typically 2-3 coats
8. Apply butyrate aluminium coat (UV barrier and filling coat)
9. Apply cellulose or 2K primer
10. Apply cellulose or 2K topcoat to match scheme

**Note on tautening dope:** Standard (tautening) nitrate dope must NOT be used
on polyester fabric. The shrinkage force added to the heat-shrunk fabric can
overstress ribs and other airframe structure. Use non-tautening nitrate only.

**Note on topcoat compatibility:** 2K topcoat over butyrate is widely used and
gives a durable finish. Cellulose is also compatible. Do not use polyurethane
systems that are incompatible with butyrate chemistry — check compatibility
before application.

## Form 205 Wording

For BGA Form 205 Rectification Worksheet, fabric covering work should be documented as:

> "Covered in accordance with Ceconite Manual 101"

Identify the fabric brand and weight used in the description of work, e.g.:
> "Recovered with Diatex 1000 polyester fabric. Finished nitrate/butyrate/
> butyrate aluminium/2K topcoat. Covered in accordance with Ceconite Manual 101."
