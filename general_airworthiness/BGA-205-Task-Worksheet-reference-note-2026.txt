## What the Form Covers

This worksheet may be used for Gliders, Motor Gliders and BGA Tugs.

Fields: Registration, Type, File Reference, Date, Check/Zone, Sheet number.
Task table: No. | Task | Action | Completed

---

## CAA Requirement — Tools and Panels Clearance

The following statement is now a CAA requirement and must be completed on every worksheet:

> "After completion of maintenance to ensure that the aircraft or component is clear
> of all tools, equipment and any extraneous parts and material, and that all access
> panels removed have been refitted."

This is no longer optional — it must be signed off on every Form 205.

## Three Signature Blocks

**Non-Part 21 aircraft** (formerly Annex II):
- Work carried out in accordance with BGA Airworthiness Exposition
- Supplement UK CAA BCAR APPROVAL: DAI/8378/73

**Part 21 aircraft** (formerly EASA aircraft):
- BGA Inspector Certificate of Release to Service ML.A.801(e)
- CAA Approval No. UK.CAO.0025
- Certifies work carried out in accordance with Part-ML

**Pilot-Owner Certificate of Release to Service**
- Ref ML.A.801(e), ML.A.201(c), ML.A.801 and ML.A.803
- For limited pilot-owner maintenance under Part-ML

Tick appropriate box.

## Form 205 Wording Guidance

When completing the task description, cite the reference document used. Examples:

- Wood repair: "Repaired in accordance with BGA Standard Repairs Section 3"
- Fabric covering: "Covered in accordance with Ceconite Manual 101"
- Adhesive joints: "Aerodux 185 resorcinol adhesive used throughout"
- Hook calibration: "Calibrated in accordance with Cair Aviation CW300/CW400 PDT"
